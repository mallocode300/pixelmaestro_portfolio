EmailJS Setup Guide for PixelMaestro Contact Form

Follow these steps to set up EmailJS to send contact form submissions to romainrossi25@gmail.com

Note: This guide will help you create an EmailJS account and obtain the credentials required for your contact form.

Step 1: Create an EmailJS Account

  1. Go to EmailJS Sign Up
  2. Create a free account (allows 200 emails/month)
  3. Verify your email address

Step 2: Create an Email Service

  1. From your EmailJS dashboard, click on "Email Services" in the sidebar
  2. Click "Add New Service"
  3. Choose your preferred email provider (Gmail, Outlook, etc.)
  4. Follow the prompts to connect your email account
  5. Note: If using Gmail, you might need to enable "Less secure apps" or create an "App Password" if you have 2FA enabled
  6. Once connected, give your service a name (e.g., "portfolio_contact")
  7. Copy the Service ID for later use

Step 3: Create an Email Template

  1. Click on "Email Templates" in the sidebar
  2. Click "Create New Template"
  3. Give your template a name (e.g., "contact_form")
  4. Set the content of your email:
    • From Name: {{from_name}}
    • Reply To: {{from_email}}
    • To Email: {{to_email}}
    • Subject: New Contact Form Message: {{subject}}
    • Body: Design your email template, using variables like {{message}}, {{from_name}}, etc.
  5. Save the template
  6. Copy the Template ID for later use

Step 6: Test Your Contact Form

  1. Run your Next.js application (npm run dev)
  2. Navigate to the Contact page
  3. Fill out the form and submit it
  4. Check that romainrossi25@gmail.com receives the email
